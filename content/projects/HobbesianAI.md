Senior thesis research applying Thomas Hobbes's political philosophy to problems of cooperation in Artificial Intelligence. The work explores how Hobbesian concepts of the state of nature and social contract theory can inform the design of multi-agent reinforcement learning systems.

Published research that bridges the gap between political philosophy and practical AI applications, demonstrating how classical political thought can guide modern technological development.
